Analysis Guidelines

Take photos of your erosion areas with a ruler or pin visible. Here's how to get good measurements:

Use a Reference Object

Put a ruler, measuring stick, or marked pin in your photo. Make sure you can see it clearly and it's standing straight up, not tilted.

Good Photo Quality

Take clear photos with good light. Don't let shadows cover your ruler or measuring stick. Try to keep the camera level.

Multiple Spots

Take photos from different parts of your farm where you see erosion. If you have a GPS, note the locations.

File Types

JPG, PNG, TIFF, or BMP photos work. Keep files under 16MB. You can upload several photos at once.

Analysis Capabilities

Measures Soil Loss

Finds your ruler or pin in the photo and calculates how much soil washed away. Accurate to within a few millimeters.

Shows Risk Areas

Colors your locations green, yellow, or red based on how bad the erosion is. Shows you where to focus your efforts.

Planting Advice

Tells you where to plant vetiver grass or other plants to stop erosion. Gives you practical steps you can actually do.

Save Your Results

Download PDF reports and data files to keep records or share with extension agents or cooperatives.
